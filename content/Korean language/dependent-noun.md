In Korean grammar, dependent nouns (의존 명사) are words that cannot stand alone and require a preceding noun or phrase for meaning, similar to how postpositions work in English. They are used to add nuance or context, like "thing" or "matter". 

## Definition
Dependent nouns are words that lack independent meaning and must be attached to another noun or phrase to convey a complete thought. 

 **Examples:**
- **것:** "thing," "matter," "it" 
- **수:** "number," "amount" 
- **뿐:** "just," "only" 
- **데:** "place," "situation" 

Units used to count things, like `마리` (animals), `그루` (trees), and `권` (books) are also considered dependent nouns.

## Usage
Dependent nouns are used to modify or clarify the meaning of the preceding noun or phrase.  They are often used in relative clauses or to express a generic stance. 

-**Examples in Sentences:**
    
- **내가 그것을 좋아해** - "I like that."
- **그것 뿐이다** - "That's all."
- **이 수는 많다** - "This number is large."
